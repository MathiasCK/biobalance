Our solution to save the rainforest.

Our solution is regrowing parts at the partly deforested rainforests to reconnect the fragments of the forest areas that are separated due to agriculture and logging. This is necessary to bring back balance of the ecosystem, to reverse the damages committed by humans, in order to stop further massive destruction that an unbalanced ecosystem will cause to the fragile biodiversity of the rainforest.

Planting tree species that belong to the rainforest on deforested grounds is not an easy task. According to botanist Charlotte Bjorå, every plant in the rainforest gets almost their entire source of nutrients from the 25 cm layer of biomass on the ground, since the ground is made of clay. Deforested grounds are missing the neutricius biomass, due to rain and extreme sunlight. In addition to this, the sunlight will kill the entirety of new vegetations planted in these conditions. Therefore we found a solution, the Rainforest Lunchbox together with large shadow cloths. The Rainforest Lunchbox is designed to decrease the death rate of planted vegetation that belong to the rainforest. The Rainforest Lunchbox does this by keeping moisture inside, while simultaneously providing the planted vegetation with all the nutrients the ground is lacking, simulating the perfect environment for the regrowing plants to thrive.

Turns unhabitable grounds habitable

As the rainforest is cut down, what is left is a barren plain of dried mud and clay whithout any source of neutriants, as the rain washes the biomass away. The cracking crust is inhabitable for any plants that belong to the tropical forest. Whith the Rainforest Foodbox, this is no longer the case.

Decrease death rate

The Rainforest Lunchbox is designed to provide neutrients, keep the humidity inside the soil and the shade cloth will protect the plants from sunlight, as they are adapted to growing in the shade of the forest. This makes the dried crust humid, and the artificual biomass that is the Rainforest Lunchbox will make it possible for our plants to grow strong.

Our Goals

  • Increase the survival rate of restored rainforest.
  • Contribute to carbon capture.
  • Create possibilities for easier and more affordable rainforest restoring that doesn't need too much maintenance.
  • Create balance in the ecosystem, to stop natural disasters in the rainforests due to unstable biodiversity
  • Create jobs for everyone who is interested in saving the rainforest. As planting the plants inside the Rainforest lunchbox is quite easy, people with developmental disabilities may contribute just as easily as any other person.

Product description

Material

PBAT, PBST and PLA, fully compostable plastic that will degenerate into nutritious biomass, H2O and release the captured CO2, which will be consumed by the trees. The plastic will also contain necessary nutritions that will be released over time. The plastic types blend nicely together, and the PLA will ensure longer durability before degeneration, before the Trees are able to live on their own. The Rainforest Luchbox will simply be eaten by its plant!

Durability

PBAT and PBST alone will decompose within two years, but the amount of PLA will ensure durability to 10 years or longer, depending on the tree species that will use the specific Rainforest Lunchboxes for.

Soil inside the Rainforest Lunchbox

The soil will contain the most fundamental nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and calcium. This we may get from sources like manure or fertilizer. Over time the trees will also get the necessary amount of nitrogen from symbiosis with different kinds of fungi, and the plants will make their own biomass on the ground. That way we may use the ecosystem to create a sustainable solution.

Economic incentives

The materials will cost approximately 32 USD for each Rainforest Lunchbox, and 400 USD each 100 kuadrat meters of shade cloths, which means we are able to plant in large areas at a cheap price. In total it would be far more expensive trying to replant rainforest without the Rainforest Lunchbox, due to the incredibly decreasing death rate of the plants when using the Rainforest Lunchbox.
